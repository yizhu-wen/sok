Paper at a Glance

Is Audio Watermarking Robust to Removal Attacks? A Comprehensive Measurement Study evaluates current audio watermarking systems under quality-preserving removal attacks for speech and music.

26watermarking schemes analyzed
10open-source methods reproduced
5speech and music datasets
127attack settings in the paper evaluation

Key takeaway: no evaluated method is robust to all tested attacks. Pitch shift, physical re-recording, and AI-induced voice conversion or TTS are major failure modes. The paper points to frame-level message repetition, broader frequency coverage, AI-distortion training, and subjective listening tests as important design and evaluation lessons.
