Paper overview

A quality-aware robustness study

The paper studies whether modern audio watermarks survive practical removal attacks while preserving the usefulness of the audio. It connects a component-wise taxonomy of 26 schemes with a benchmark of 10 reproducible methods across speech and music. We answer three research questions:

RQ1. How do traditional and AI-based methods compare in robustness? Answer: AI-based methods are more robust on average, defending against 51.63% of evaluated attack settings versus 28.97% for traditional methods.
RQ2. What are the main robustness challenges of existing methods? Answer: Physical and AI-induced distortions remain the main challenges, with pitch shift, time stretch, physical re-recording, VC, and TTS as major failure modes.
RQ3. How do architectural design choices affect robustness? Answer: Message repetition and broad frequency-range distribution improve robustness against digital and physical attacks, while AI-distortion training helps against AI-induced attacks.

Pitch shift and time stretch break watermark alignment

Pitch shift and time stretch can lower bit recovery accuracy while keeping speech or music useful under selected quality thresholds. In this study, the quality-preservation thresholds are ViSQOL > 3.0, MUSHRA-Music > 79, and MUSHRA-Speech > 60.

Physical re-recording is a major failure mode

Re-recording introduces room, distance, microphone, and speaker effects. Distance causes larger accuracy drops than device changes.
